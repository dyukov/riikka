Nudging Aalto University community for inclusion


Master’s thesis in Service Design

Riikka Jääskeläinen 12/2019


International Aalto

Aalto University is one of the most international universities, ranking 50th in the world (32nd in Europe) by the internationality of its student and staff population, learning environment, research and knowledge transfer (Times Higher Education 2019).

The research setting

The topics of international students’ inclusion and employability have received considerable attention in Finland. Highly skilled experts are needed to boost the economy, though roughly 45 % of international graduates are employed in Finland. However, the results of the recent wellbeing student survey at Aalto University (AllWell? 2018-19) show that though international students do well in their studies, they feel lower levels of inclusion compared to their local peers. The inclusion of a student in higher education has both an academic and a social dimension (Tinto 1975). 


My research questions are

 

1. What kind of challenges do international, degree pursuing Bachelor and Master students encounter, especially when starting their studies at Aalto University?


2. What type of information and skills would the new international students benefit from in order to succeed in their studies in Aalto University and at the start their new life in Finland? 


3. What kind of new service could respond to the students’ needs, thus improving their experience, help them succeed in their studies and increase their sense of belonging in the Aalto University community?

The methods used in this study

  • Student survey 2018-19 (fee-liable, international students, N=118)
  • Indepth interviews 2019 with 6 international Master's students
  • Mobile ethnography 2019 with 7 new international students
  • Customer journey map & key insights
  • Co-creative workshops with Aalto students and staff
  • Storyboard
  • Stakeholder map
  • Concept & prototype
  • Service blueprint
  • Value proposition canvas

User-centered approach

The study has been done by using different methods of service design and co-creation practises with Aalto students and staff. The focus of the study is on the international students' experience.
